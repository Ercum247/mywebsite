Why The Aegean Coast

Sun, salt air, and centuries of practice.

The hills along Turkey's Aegean coast have grown olives for thousands of years. Long, dry summers and mineral-rich coastal soil produce fruit with a distinct character — grassy, slightly peppery, balanced rather than sharp.

It's the same coastline that shapes the region's wine, its herbs, and its cooking. Buyers who already know Mediterranean oils tend to recognize it immediately; buyers who don't usually ask for the name again before they finish tasting it.
